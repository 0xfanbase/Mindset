## Method

1. **Diff + full-file read** — the complete `e2913a9` diff, the current `assets/favicon.svg`,
   `manifest.webmanifest`, `sw.js`, the BUILD-PLAN.md v1.21 changelog and amended C.2 note, the
   decisions.md v1.21 entry, and `cebb1e6`'s fold-in against my own v1.20 write-up.
2. **Source inspection** — both uploaded originals viewed in full (6240×4160 Sony, EXIF
   orientation 6; 3024×4032 iPhone), plus the third upload: the owner's original bug-report
   screenshot, which shows exactly the black home-screen tile with the drop badge stranded
   top-left that the v1.20 audit predicted from pixel forensics — the founding defect of this
   whole icon thread, now confirmed photographically.
3. **Full pipeline reproduction, to the byte** — sources copied beside a generated HTML file,
   rendered via the same globally-installed Playwright/Chromium; the split-frame recipe
   recovered by grid-searching `object-position` against the lossless 180 px PNG as oracle,
   after measuring the divider's exact columns and color from the committed file.
4. **The logged `page.setContent()` gotcha re-tested empirically** — `setContent` +
   `file://` image: `naturalWidth` 0; `page.goto("file://…")`: 4160×6240 (which also proves
   Chromium applies the Sony photo's EXIF rotation — the composite depends on it).
5. **Metadata forensics** — JPEG marker walk (every APPn/COM segment enumerated) on both
   committed JPEGs and the favicon's embedded JPEG; PNG chunk walk on `apple-touch-icon.png`;
   quantization-table comparison to pin the claimed qualities; GPS scan of the sources.
6. **Verifier ratchet, both sides** — `verify.mjs all` at `e2913a9` (63/63) and, via a
   temporary worktree since removed, at parent `cebb1e6` (63/63); per-check name lists diffed:
   identical. `scripts/verify.mjs` itself has a zero-line diff across the commit.
7. **Mechanical checks** — `node --check sw.js`; independent normalize-and-compare of `sw.js`
   vs Appendix C.2 modulo `ASSETS`; budgets via `wc -c`/`du -cb`/`file`; grep sweeps (stale
   `.png` references, PII patterns, `localStorage`, `max-width`); no-new-deps filesystem check.
8. **Visual verification** — true-pixel small-size sheet of the real tab icon (`favicon.svg`
   at 16/32/48/64 px with nearest-neighbor blowups), iOS-squircle and Android-circle launcher
   sims on dark and light with an 80%-safe-circle overlay, a direct render of the committed
   SVG (clipPath verified working), and 2× zoom sweeps over every background zone of the
   committed 512 for the PII pass — all actually looked at.

## Findings

### Confirmed (implementer claims that survived independent verification)

- **Every committed raster artifact is byte-identical to what the described pipeline
  produces.** With the recipe recovered from the lossless PNG — `object-position: 50% 50%`
  (orange, left) and `52% 50%` (grey, right), 6 px divider in exactly `rgb(250,249,245)` =
  `#FAF9F5` = calm `--bg` (uniform columns 87–92 at 180 px), full-bleed square, `deviceScaleFactor`
  1 — my fresh renders reproduce `apple-touch-icon.png` (51,038 B), `icon-192.jpg` (10,915 B)
  and `icon-512.jpg` (51,573 B) **byte-for-byte**, JPEG quality 90 included. The favicon's
  embedded JPEG also reproduces byte-for-byte as the same composite rendered at 128 px with a
  4 px divider at quality 78 (quantization tables match a known-q78 encode exactly). Nothing
  was hand-edited anywhere in the chain, and the JPEG-quality and byte-count claims are exact,
  not approximate.
- **Metadata hygiene is complete.** Committed JPEGs and the embedded favicon JPEG carry only
  JFIF + Chromium's standard Google-sRGB ICC profile — no EXIF, no GPS, no comment segments;
  the PNG is IHDR/IDAT/IEND only. Nothing of the sources' camera metadata (Sony body model,
  capture dates) survives into the repo; the sources themselves contain no GPS tags either.
- **All byte/budget claims recompute exactly (invariant 6).** Icons total 113,526 B ≤ 150 KB
  (the three files only; old PNGs genuinely deleted, no strays in `assets/icons/`);
  `favicon.svg` 6,618 B with the embedded JPEG at the claimed 4,744 B → 6,328 base64 chars,
  128×128 per its SOF header; page weight 178,632 B ≤ 350 KB (manifest +2 B from the MIME
  change — the only page-set file that moved); JS 26,691 B ≤ 60 KB; fonts blob-identical.
  Dimensions via `file`: 180×180 PNG, 192×192 and 512×512 JPEG, as claimed.
- **Manifest and format mechanics are right.** `.jpg` sources now correctly declare
  `"type": "image/jpeg"`; `purpose: "any maskable"` carried over; no stale reference to the
  deleted PNG filenames anywhere in live code; `index.html` untouched, its favicon and
  apple-touch links still valid because filenames and types were deliberately preserved. The
  changelog's claim that the icons-budget check is extension-agnostic is code-accurate
  (`readdirSync` sum, no filter). The 355,512 B lossless-PNG figure that motivated JPEG was
  not re-derived here, but its order of magnitude is self-evidently right for a 512 px photo
  and the conclusion (PNG busts a 150 KB three-file budget) follows regardless.
- **The `setContent` gotcha is real and correctly diagnosed.** Reproduced both halves:
  broken images under `setContent` (non-`file://` document origin), clean loads via
  `page.goto("file://…")`. Worth having logged.
- **Verifier ratchet honored (invariant 12).** 63/63 at `cebb1e6` and `e2913a9`, identical
  check-name lists, `verify.mjs` untouched — the unchanged count is the same 63 checks.
- **`sw.js` v7 is byte-identical to the amended C.2 modulo `ASSETS`** (verify's check plus my
  own normalization); `node --check` clean. The v6→v7 bump is the correct call for the same
  reason as v1.20 — `favicon.svg`'s bytes changed and Chromium-family tab-favicon fetches
  bypass the SW `fetch` handler, so install-time `addAll` is the only reliable refresh — and
  the C.2 rationale note now carries that reasoning verbatim, correctly attributed.
- **PII sweep of the committed pixels: clean, confirmed independently (invariant 1).** What
  actually ships, inspected at 2× zoom zone by zone: left half — heavily blurred window
  blinds with no exterior view resolvable, a plain wooden box edge, bare wall, a generic
  blanket sliver; right half — featureless dark-room blur, wood flooring, and a small red
  sliver of an X-frame furniture leg. No text, documents, screens, reflections, house
  numbers, or anything else in invariant 1's enumerated categories or adjacent to them. The
  full-resolution sources (which contain strictly more detail than any committed crop) were
  also inspected and are equally clean. Two cats at home, nothing more.
- **Remaining invariants: clean.** Zero new dependencies (no `node_modules`/`package.json`/
  lockfile; Playwright used from `/opt` as before); no CSS/JS/data files touched
  (`git diff` empty across `scripts/`, `data/`, `styles.css`, `index.html`, `app.js`,
  `figure.js`, `lib.mjs`); `localStorage` appears only in prose; `noindex` and `.nojekyll`
  untouched; card/value data untouched so invariant 2 is unaffected.

### Corrected (claims that did not fully survive; none change the verdict)

- **"Both faces read clearly at every size down to 32px" — true for one cat, not both.** On
  true-pixel renders of the real tab icon, the grey cat still reads as a cat at 32 px (eyes,
  nose, face shape all present). The orange half does not: its source is an environmental
  shot, so the face is roughly 7 px tall at icon-32 and dissolves into a warm interior blur —
  it becomes findable around 48 px and clear from 64 px up. Since a standard 2× browser tab
  renders `favicon.svg` at exactly 32 device px, the shipping tab icon reads as "warm diptych
  with a cat on the right", not "two cats". Every home-screen size — the surface the owner
  actually asked about — is fine. The changelog sentence should be corrected to say the grey
  face holds to 32 px and the orange face to ~48 px.
- **"A small 128px JPEG crop of the same composite" — it is a re-render, not a crop, and the
  divider quietly changed width.** Byte-proven: the embedded favicon JPEG is the composite
  recipe re-rendered at 128 px with a **4 px** divider (the icons use 6 px), quality 78.
  Reproducing the literal claim — same composite, 6 px divider — yields 4,702 B, not the
  committed 4,744 B, which would send a future auditor chasing a phantom discrepancy. The
  4 px choice is arguably better (6 px would be proportionally chunkier at 128), but it is
  documented nowhere; one clause in the changelog fixes it.

### Minor, non-blocking observations

- **The divider's relative width varies by surface.** Fixed CSS pixels per render mean the
  divider is ~1.2% of width on the 512 (Android) and ~3.3% on the 180 (iOS) — the same
  logical mark ships with a hairline on one platform and a visibly chunkier seam on the
  other. Side by side in the sims it is noticeable; on separate phones it never will be. If
  it is ever revisited, a proportional divider (~2% everywhere) would unify them — not worth
  a regeneration on its own.
- **`preserveAspectRatio="xMidYMid slice"` on the favicon's `<image>` is a no-op** — the
  embedded JPEG is already exactly 128×128 in a 128×128 frame. Harmless belt-and-braces.
- **Maskable honesty appreciated, and verified anyway.** The changelog deliberately claims
  only that both faces sit in the vertical-center band, explicitly disclaiming v1.20-style
  safe-zone math — accurate, and the right epistemics for a full-bleed photo. Under the
  worst-case circular mask both faces remain fully visible (verified in the sim); only outer
  ear/cheek edges graze the 80% guarantee circle. Nothing to fix.
- **The PNG/JPEG asymmetry is sound.** Keeping `apple-touch-icon.png` as PNG tracks
  long-standing iOS flakiness with non-PNG touch icons (industry lore this sandbox cannot
  re-verify live, but the downside of PNG here is 51 KB — cheap insurance on exactly the file
  the owner's bug report was about), while JPEG for the two manifest icons is fully supported
  on the Android/Chromium surfaces that consume them. No real risk identified.
- **Budget headroom shrank from ~75% free to ~26% free** (38,335 → 113,526 B of 153,600).
  Fine today; the next icon-family addition (say, a monochrome themed icon) still fits, but
  the icon budget is no longer effectively unlimited.
- **The founding bug is now photographically confirmed.** The owner's uploaded screenshot
  shows the exact black-tile-with-corner-badge failure the v1.20 audit reconstructed from the
  Stage-5 PNGs' alpha statistics. The causal chain — broken tiles since Stage 5, owner
  notices, lion fixes the pipeline, cats personalize the mark — is now fully evidenced end
  to end.

### Design judgment (does a two-photo diptych actually work as this app's icon?)

At home-screen sizes — the ask — yes, and better than I expected a split-frame to. The two
halves are genuinely balanced: both cats' faces sit on the same upper-center optical band,
both photos share a warm domestic palette that sits comfortably next to the app's calm theme,
and the hairline `--bg`-colored divider is the same reuse-the-app's-own-tokens move that made
the lion's blossom-accent heart feel native — it stitches the two photos into one deliberate
object rather than two images jammed together. The pairing even has a nice editorial rhythm:
one cat lounging and sidelong, one sitting alert and face-on — it reads as "portraits of a
pair", which is precisely what was asked for. On the dark-wallpaper sims (iOS squircle,
Android circle) it is warm and instantly recognizable as *their* cats, which is the entire
point of replacing an illustration with photos; no generic mark can compete with that for a
personal app, and the composite execution — divider, framing band, color discipline — is as
close to "intentional diptych" as a no-zoom split-frame can get.

The one real aesthetic tension is subject scale: the grey half is a tight portrait, the
orange half an environmental shot, so the grey cat dominates at small sizes (see Corrected).
The recipe has no zoom knob — `object-fit: cover` pins scale to full height, leaving only
horizontal pan — so a tighter orange face would need a pre-cropped source or a scaled-`<img>`
recipe. That asymmetry is invisible at 120–180 px and reads as variety, not error. If the
owner cares that the *tab* icon shows two cats rather than one cat plus warm abstraction, the
cheap fix is favicon-only: re-crop the orange half tighter (or feature the grey cat alone) in
the 128 px embed, leaving the home-screen icons untouched. Owner's call; not a defect.

## Verdict

**Ship.** This is the strongest verification result of the three icon audits: every committed
raster artifact reproduces **byte-for-byte** from the stated pipeline and recovered recipe, the
metadata is surgically clean, the PII sweep of the actual shipped pixels (and the richer
sources behind them) finds nothing in or near invariant 1's scope, the verifier is 63/63 with
identical check lists on both sides, and all budgets recompute with room to spare. Fold two
textual corrections back into the source of truth per convention: (1) the 32 px legibility
sentence — grey face holds to 32 px, orange to ~48 px, tab icon reads one-cat-plus-warmth at
2× tab size; (2) the favicon embed is a re-render at 128 px with a 4 px divider, not a "crop"
of the 6 px composite. One optional, owner's-choice follow-up: a tighter orange half in the
favicon embed only, if two-cats-at-tab-size matters to them. Nothing blocks the merge.
