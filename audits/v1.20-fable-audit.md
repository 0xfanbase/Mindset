# v1.20 — Fable-led project-director audit (lion + hearts app icon)

Requested by the owner alongside the feature itself: replace the plain blue water-drop app/
shortcut icon with a lion with hearts, with a Fable project-director review before it ships —
the same live-gate pattern as v1.17. Scope: the v1.20 diff on branch
`claude/website-logo-shortcut-fmgyu9` (touches `assets/favicon.svg`, the three PNGs under
`assets/icons/`, `sw.js`, `BUILD-PLAN.md`, `audits/decisions.md`). Per this project's audit
convention (v1.5, v1.17), every implementer claim was re-verified independently — the safe-zone
geometry recomputed by brute force from the raw SVG coordinates, the PNGs re-rendered from
scratch and pixel-compared, verifier counts re-run at both the feature commit and the parent
via a temporary worktree, the heart glyph's provenance checked against the upstream source —
nothing was accepted on the implementer's word.

Note on repo state: mid-audit, the commit was re-created — `1fd47e9` became `b9ed663`.
Verified harmless before proceeding: both commits carry the identical tree (`8fe49ac1`), the
identical message and parent (`80dd933`); only the author/committer timestamps (+254 s) and
the commit's SSH signature differ, and neither hash was ever pushed, so invariant 10 (no
rewrites of pushed history) is untouched. Every finding below applies to `b9ed663` as-is, and
the working tree is clean against it.

## Method

1. **Diff + full-file read** — the complete v1.20 diff plus the current `assets/favicon.svg`,
   `sw.js`, `manifest.webmanifest`, `scripts/verify.mjs`, the relevant `index.html` head, the
   BUILD-PLAN.md v1.20 changelog and Appendix C.2, the decisions.md v1.20 entry, and the
   Stage-5-era icon entries (decisions.md 2026-07-13, FINAL-AUDIT.md) they cite.
2. **Independent geometry recomputation** — max radial distance from the canvas center
   computed in Node by brute force over the *full boundary* of all 8 rotated mane ellipses
   (720,000 samples each), not just their tips, plus closed-form confirmation; same for ears,
   face disc, and the heart's transformed bounding box; rounded-rect coverage of the inscribed
   circle; and the old drop path's max extent (Bézier-sampled) for the comparison claim.
3. **Independent re-render + pixel comparison** — the globally-installed Playwright (absolute
   path, nothing added to the repo) driving the pre-installed headless Chromium: fresh renders
   of the committed SVG at 512/192/180 px, canvas-diffed pixel-by-pixel against the three
   committed PNGs; alpha/corner/center pixel probes of both the new and the old (parent-commit)
   PNGs; a true-pixel legibility sheet at 16/32/48/64 px with nearest-neighbor blowups; and
   home-screen simulations — iOS-style squircle mask on dark and light wallpapers, Android
   circular mask, and an 80%-safe-circle overlay on the raw 512 — all actually looked at.
4. **Verifier ratchet check, both sides** — `node scripts/verify.mjs all` at the feature
   commit (63/63) *and*, via a temporary git worktree, at the parent `80dd933` (63/63, and
   also the merge-base with `main`). The per-check name lists from both runs were diffed:
   identical, so the unchanged count is genuinely the same 63 checks, not a coincidence —
   consistent with `verify.mjs` having a zero-line diff in this commit.
5. **Mechanical checks** — `node --check sw.js`; my own normalize-and-compare of `sw.js`
   against Appendix C.2 modulo the `ASSETS` array (independent of the stage5 check that
   enforces the same thing); byte budgets measured directly with `wc -c`/`du -cb`/`file`;
   grep sweeps of the diff for PII patterns, quote glyphs, `localStorage`, `max-width`; a
   filesystem check that no `node_modules`/`package.json`/lockfile appeared; and a fetch of
   Google's `material-design-icons` `favorite` 24px SVG to settle the heart path's provenance.

## Findings

### The headline finding — the old PNGs were never valid tiles, and this commit silently fixes them

Probing the parent commit's PNGs: in all three, the drop artwork occupies only a 64×64 patch
in the top-left corner (content bounding box exactly [0,0,63,63] — the SVG's intrinsic size),
with an identical 3,869 opaque pixels per file and everything else fully transparent — 88% of
the 180 canvas, ~98.5% of the 512. The center pixel of the old "512×512 icon" is transparent.
On a real home screen that renders as: iOS, a black tile (iOS composites transparent
apple-touch-icons over black) with a tiny drop in one corner; Android maskable, a mostly-empty
circle with the drop largely cropped away. This shipped at Stage 5 (2026-07-13) and was never
caught — FINAL-AUDIT checked that icons were *present* and within budget, not what they
contained. The failure signature matches the repo's own v1.6 note about the raw
`--headless --screenshot` CLI (viewport not applied to layout; an SVG document renders at its
intrinsic 64 px, top-left, on a transparent canvas). The v1.20 pipeline — an `<img>` scaled to
each exact target size on an opaque page — is what produced correct full-bleed tiles. Two
prose consequences, folded into Corrected below: the changelog's "same pipeline Stage 5 used
originally" and "the static icon had shipped as a plain teardrop mark, unchanged, since
Stage 5" are both wrong in the way that matters. One bonus: the new PNGs are RGB with no alpha
channel (corners opaque white), which removes the iOS black-fill hazard class entirely.

### Confirmed (implementer claims that survived independent verification)

- **The committed PNGs are exactly what the committed SVG renders to.** Fresh Chromium renders
  at 180/192/512 differ from the committed files by **zero pixels** (0/32,400, 0/36,864,
  0/262,144; max channel delta 0). The "regenerated, not hand-edited" claim holds at the
  strongest possible level. Dimensions via `file`: 180×180, 192×192, 512×512, as claimed.
- **The safe-zone math is exact.** Brute force over all 8 mane locks' full boundaries gives a
  max radial distance of **24.5000** units (76.56% of the 32-unit half-width), inside the
  25.6-unit (80%) Android maskable safe circle — and the maximum genuinely occurs at each
  lock's outer tip, so the implementer's tips-only check was sufficient (their by-construction
  argument — rotation about the center is an isometry, so one lock checks all eight — is
  sound; I verified the stronger full-boundary statement anyway). Ears reach 23.30, face disc
  14.50, heart bbox corner 15.78 — all inside. The heart is horizontally centered (x 25→39 on
  a 64 canvas). The background rect's rx=14 corners still fully cover the inscribed circle, so
  a circular mask shows no gap. The old drop's max extent was 22.0 (68.75%) — the changelog's
  "cleared the same margin by a wider, un-measured amount" is true.
- **Verifier ratchet honored (invariant 12).** 63/63 at parent `80dd933` (temp worktree,
  since removed) and 63/63 at the feature commit, with byte-identical check-name lists;
  `verify.mjs` itself has a zero-line diff. No check added, removed, or retargeted — the
  changelog's reading that no ratchet entry was required is correct, and the decisions.md
  entry that was written anyway (for the cache bump) is a bonus, not an obligation.
- **`sw.js` is byte-identical to Appendix C.2 modulo `ASSETS`** — confirmed by the stage5
  check *and* by my own independent normalization; `CACHE` is `"mindset-v6"` on both sides;
  `node --check` clean. `ASSETS` still lists `./assets/favicon.svg` and (correctly) none of
  the PNGs — the OS fetches those directly at add-to-home-screen time.
- **Budgets all pass (invariant 6), measured directly:** icons 38,335 B — exactly the changelog
  figure, and the same three files verify.mjs's whole-directory sum sees — ≤150 KB; JS total
  26,691 B ≤ 60 KB (`figure.js` 9,554 B ≤ 12 KB, blob-unchanged); fonts 178,420 B ≤ 300 KB
  (all four woff2 blobs hash-identical across the diff); page weight 178,630 B ≤ 350 KB. One
  precision note: the page-weight check's file set contains no icons at all, so "page-weight
  green at the new byte counts" was never in doubt — true, but vacuously.
- **The color claims are real tokens.** `#FAF9F5` is calm `--bg` and `#B84870` is blossom
  `--accent`, verbatim from `styles.css` — the heart genuinely reuses the app's own accent
  rather than an invented pink, the same reuse-real-tokens instinct as v1.17's pill.
- **Cache-bump judgment call: right call, and the case is stronger than the implementer made
  it.** C.2's historical rationale ties bumps to the `ASSETS` *list* changing; here only a
  listed file's bytes changed, and the entry honestly flags the bump as judgment. I agree with
  bumping, for a reason the entry only gestures at: Chromium-family browsers fetch tab
  favicons outside the service-worker fetch handler, so the "network-first self-heals
  eventually" fallback is not actually guaranteed for `favicon.svg` — install-time `addAll`
  under a new cache name is the only reliable way to refresh the offline shell's copy, and
  `activate` then purges `mindset-v5`. Cost is a one-time ~330 KB shell re-fetch for a
  personal-scale user base. Documenting it in C.2's own rationale note was the right
  placement.
- **Remaining invariants: clean.** No PII patterns anywhere in the diff (0 hits); no card or
  value data touched, so invariant 2's scope is untouched (the straight quotes in the new
  changelog/decisions prose are code spans and scare quotes, consistent with the document's
  existing style — invariant 2 governs card bodies, which verify.mjs still confirms clean);
  zero new runtime deps (no `node_modules`, no `package.json`, no lockfile anywhere;
  Playwright was used from `/opt/node22/...`, outside the repo); no CSS touched and no
  `max-width` query exists; the diff's one `localStorage` mention is prose; `noindex` meta and
  `.nojekyll` present and untouched; `manifest.webmanifest` and `index.html`'s icon links
  correctly untouched (files swapped in place, all references still valid, all URLs relative).

### Corrected (claims that did not fully survive; none change the verdict)

- **"Icons total 38,335 bytes (was ~16KB before)" — the old figure is wrong by ~2×.** The
  parent commit's three PNGs sum to **7,902 bytes**. The "~16KB" traces to the Stage-5
  decisions entry and FINAL-AUDIT, which measured disk blocks (`du` without `-b`: three ≤4 KiB
  files round up to 12 KiB, plus 4 KiB for the directory = 16 KiB), while the new 38,335 is an
  apparent-size byte count — two different bases compared as if one. Real growth is 4.9×, not
  2.4×; immaterial to the budget either way, but BUILD-PLAN.md should not carry a number a
  future auditor can't reproduce (v1.17 precedent, verbatim).
- **"Same pipeline Stage 5 used originally" / "shipped as a plain teardrop mark, unchanged,
  since Stage 5" — both need rewording per the headline finding.** Same rasterizer *engine*,
  materially different invocation — and the difference is precisely what fixed three silently
  malformed shipped files. The teardrop claim is true of the SVG only; the PNG derivatives
  never displayed as a proper drop tile on any phone. The changelog should say so — this
  commit deserves more credit than it claims, and the record should stop implying the old
  icons worked.
- **"An original geometric mark" — one qualifier required.** The composition (mane, ears,
  face, layout) is original, but the heart-nose path is the Material Design `favorite` glyph
  **coordinate-for-coordinate** (verified against Google's `material-design-icons` source; the
  repo copy is the comma-formatted variant of the identical path data). This is fine to ship —
  Apache-2.0, a near-generic shape, sensible engineering versus hand-rolling heart Béziers,
  and no project invariant touches it (invariant 2 governs prose quotes in card bodies, not
  vector paths) — but the source of truth shouldn't call the mark original without noting the
  one stock component.

### Minor, non-blocking observations

- **16 px is past the mark's legibility floor.** At 32 px and up the lion-with-heart reads
  clearly (verified on true-pixel renders, not scaled screenshots); at 16 px it collapses to a
  warm rosette with a pink center — charming, but not identifiably a lion. Acceptable: browser
  tabs get `favicon.svg` (not these PNGs) and at 2× device pixels (32 px) it holds; every
  home-screen size the owner actually asked about — 180/192/512 — is fully legible. The old
  drop was more legible at 16 px; that is the honest cost of a face over a silhouette.
- **The flower adjacency is real but managed.** Eight petal-shaped locks around a disc is one
  ear-removal away from a sunflower — the implementer's own decisions entry admits round one
  read as a flower. The shipped version escapes it via the elongated two-tone locks and
  especially the ears; brown-on-tan also reads animal, not botanical. My eyes at 48–512 px:
  lion first, flower second. At 16 px the flower reading wins, per the previous point.
- **The manifest's `"purpose": "any maskable"` single-icon pattern is pre-existing** and was
  consciously accepted in FINAL-AUDIT §5 back when the mark was a simple drop. The acceptance
  still holds — arguably better now, since the safe-zone margin is verified by math rather
  than assumed — but if the icon ever gains full-bleed art, split `any` and `maskable`.
- **The decisions entry's three-round iteration narrative is outside the repo** (scratch-dir
  renders) and can't be independently verified. It's plausible and consistent with the final
  geometry, and nothing load-bearing rests on it — the verifiable claims (pixel-exact
  regeneration, safe-zone math) all reproduced.

### Design judgment (does it actually read as "a lion with hearts"?)

Yes — with one interpretive liberty the owner should knowingly bless. The mark is a lion's
face whose nose is a single heart: at every real home-screen size it reads instantly as a
warm, slightly toy-like lion, and the heart is unmistakably a heart, structural rather than
stuck on. The palette is the app's own voice — calm-theme background, blossom accent for the
heart, flat shapes, no gradients or outlines — so the icon and the app feel like one object,
which is exactly what the v1.17 audit praised in the countdown pill. Against the (nominally)
blue drop it replaces, it trades cool minimalism for warmth without tipping into kitsch: the
`TODAY`-pill school of restraint, applied to a lion. The dark-wallpaper simulation is the
flattering one — the pale tile and warm mane sit beautifully on black.

The liberty: the owner said "hearts", plural; the mark contains exactly one. I endorse the
fold-in — at 16–32 px two competing motifs would smear into noise, and a heart *as* the nose
is a stronger idea than a heart *next to* a lion — but it is an interpretation, not a
transcription, and the implementer's changelog frames it as design cleverness without flagging
that the count changed. If the owner wants plural literally, the cheapest faithful upgrade is
heart-shaped ear inners: legible hearts at 180 px+, degrading gracefully to the current dots
at favicon sizes. That is a follow-up option, not a defect.

## Verdict

**Ship.** Every load-bearing claim survived independent re-verification — pixel-exact PNGs,
exact safe-zone math, identical 63/63 verifier runs on both sides of the change, sw.js/C.2
identity, all budgets and invariants clean — and the commit quietly repairs a real shipped
defect it doesn't even claim credit for: the home-screen PNGs had been malformed (64 px art on
a transparent canvas) since Stage 5. Two follow-ups, both textual, per the v1.17 convention
that corrections get folded back into the source of truth: (1) fix the v1.20 changelog's
"~16KB before" (7,902 bytes; du block-size artifact, also present in the Stage-5/FINAL-AUDIT
records), and reword "same pipeline"/"unchanged teardrop" to record that the old PNGs were
broken and this regeneration fixed them; (2) qualify "original geometric mark" to note the
heart path is the stock Material favorite glyph. And one sentence for the owner, not the repo:
the design delivers one heart, not several, by deliberate choice — say the word and the ear
inners become hearts too. Nothing blocks the merge.
